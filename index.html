Corporate Timeline - Key Events
1997
Todd Hornbeck co-founds Hornbeck Offshore. First newbuild program of proprietary deepwater OSVs.
2004
IPO on NYSE under ticker HOS. Becomes only publicly traded company focused on deepwater OSVs in the GoA.
2006-2014
Expansion through five newbuild programs. Fleet grows to 60+ vessels. Peak deepwater boom period.
2014-2019
Oil price collapse from $100+ to sub-$30. Prolonged downturn, vessel stacking, NYSE delisting (Dec 2019).
May 2020
Chapter 11 bankruptcy filing. $1.2B+ in debt restructured.
Sep 2020
Emerges from bankruptcy as private entity. $100M new equity from Ares, Whitebox, Highbridge.
Jan 2022 - Feb 2023
Acquires 16 high-spec OSVs from Edison Chouest in two transactions. Fleet rebuild accelerates.
Oct 2023
Settles MPSV construction dispute. Two 400-class newbuilds to be completed by Eastern Shipbuilding.
Dec 2023
Files S-1 for IPO. Plans to relist on NYSE under "HOS."
Nov 2025
HOS Rocinante (first U.S.-flagged SOV) delivered. Placed on offshore wind contract immediately.
Jan 2026
Strategic partnership with Saronic Technologies for autonomous vessel technology.
Mar 2026
$291.8M Navy T-AGSE contract renewed (sole-source). Base period + options through Aug 2031.
